About Me

I am Pooria Azami, a Master’s student specializing in deep learning at Yazd University, with a particular focus on computer vision. My research centers on representation learning for computer vision applications, especially the development of effective representations for 3D human pose estimation.

I am enthusiastic about collaborating on deep learning research projects, particularly those involving 3D and 4D scene reconstruction. I am also interested in applying computer vision and machine learning techniques to medical imaging and healthcare-related problems.

Beyond my research interests, I enjoy studying mathematics. In particular, I am fascinated by topology, manifold theory, group theory, probability theory, statistics—especially Bayesian statistics—and linear algebra.

I am highly self-motivated and continuously strive to deepen my knowledge by reading and analyzing research papers whenever time permits.

I also enjoy teaching machine learning and deep learning concepts to motivated students and helping them develop a strong understanding of the underlying principles.
